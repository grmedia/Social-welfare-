What is Child Abuse?

Child abuse includes any action that causes harm to a child, whether physical, emotional, or sexual.

  • Physical harm or injury
  • Emotional or psychological abuse
  • Sexual abuse or exploitation
  • Neglect or abandonment
  • Child labor or trafficking
WhatsApp Info

Signs of Abuse

Recognizing the signs of abuse is the first step to protecting a child.

  • Unexplained injuries or bruises
  • Sudden changes in behavior
  • Fear of certain people or places
  • Difficulty sleeping or nightmares
  • Decline in school performance
  • Inappropriate sexual behavior or knowledge
More Info

What To Do

If you suspect a child is being abused, it's important to take action.

  • Listen to and believe the child
  • Report to a trusted adult or authority
  • Contact social welfare services
  • Ensure the child's immediate safety
  • Provide emotional support
Report Abuse Now Emergency: 999

Online Safety

Protecting children in the digital space is increasingly important.

  • Monitor online activities
  • Teach about privacy settings
  • Discuss online risks and scams
  • Encourage open communication
  • Set clear boundaries for internet use
Safety Tips

Safe Environments

Creating safe spaces for children at home, school, and in the community.

  • Establish clear rules and boundaries
  • Supervise children appropriately
  • Teach children about personal safety
  • Build trusting relationships
  • Know the signs of grooming

Emotional Support

Helping children recover from trauma and build resilience.

  • Provide consistent emotional support
  • Encourage expression of feelings
  • Seek professional counseling when needed
  • Build self-esteem and confidence
  • Create stable routines

Legal Framework

Understanding the laws and policies that protect children in Zimbabwe

Constitution of Zimbabwe

The Constitution provides the foundation for children's rights in Zimbabwe.

  • Section 19: Children's rights
  • Section 81: Specific rights of children
  • Protection from economic and sexual exploitation
  • Right to education, healthcare, and shelter
Read Constitution

Children's Act

The Children's Act [Chapter 5:06] provides detailed provisions for child protection.

  • Definition of a child (under 18 years)
  • Provisions against child abuse and neglect
  • Procedures for child protection cases
  • Establishment of children's courts
Read Act

Domestic Violence Act

Protects children from domestic violence and provides for protection orders.

  • Definition of domestic violence
  • Protection orders for victims
  • Criminal penalties for perpetrators
  • Support services for affected children
Get Help

International Conventions

Zimbabwe is signatory to international conventions protecting children's rights.

  • UN Convention on the Rights of the Child
  • African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child
  • ILO Conventions on child labor
  • Optional Protocols on child trafficking
UN Convention

Education Act

Regulates the education system and ensures children's right to education.

  • Free and compulsory basic education
  • Regulations on school discipline
  • Protection from discrimination in education
  • Special education needs provisions
Education Help

Public Health Act

Provides for children's health rights and access to healthcare services.

  • Immunization requirements
  • Child and maternal health services
  • Nutrition programs for children
  • Adolescent health services
Health Help
